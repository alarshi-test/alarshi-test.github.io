The cratonic interior of the Central and Eastern US (CEUS) hosts several intraplate seismic zones, including the New Madrid Seismic Zone (largest intraplate seismic zone of the US).
I am interested in the effects of the tomography-based thermal and chemical heterogeneity in the upper mantle on the crustal faults of the CEUS. In particular, I examine the effects of the low-velocity zone below the New Madrid Seismic Zone and the foundering lithosphere below the Eastern Tennessee Seismic Zone using three-dimensional numerical models. I am interested in using S-to-P conversions in the Mississippi Embayment to find the lithospheric structure. Due to the gradual velocity change in the cratonic interior, I calculate stacked receiver functions over the past twenty years recored at the dense USArray station network. I invert the stacked receiver function to infer the best-fit velocity model.
